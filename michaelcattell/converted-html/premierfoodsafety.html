About the project

www.premierfoodsafety.com

Food safety in America is a big business, all companies (food based – restaurants etc) need to have a trained food handler, this is where Premier Food Safety come in.

They are one of the largest and most reputable providers of food safety education in the United States, with 35+ years behind them and a client base which boasts of over 250,000 and counting and clients such as McDonalds, Panda Express and Subway.

They needed planning, website, plus online training to be designed and built, slowly and gradually; so not to disrupt the current users – average traffic to the site is 600 users.

My Role

Lead Designer, showcasing and explaining modern techniques to the CEO of the company, ensuring a new brand direction to be kept consistent. Branding was reinvented and controlled in print, online and internally.

The Layout

Clean, simple and very assessable to all levels of users, with ages ranging from 16-80, male and female, entry level to management – we had to make all print and web work straightforward, which, for a product that can and is often accepted as being quite complicated, I think we achieved this.

Print is dead – not according to real companies

An important part of this company’s revenue is via incoming calls, sending out a flyer every quarter therefore is make or break – my task was to un-complicate quite a complicated flyer! I made the pricing as clear as possible, and also directed the user’s eye to right side of the flyer (the manager would look to the left as that bit is relevant, but the right side is more for the entry level employees).

Putting it all together

On-going project – to be finished before November 2013. Website to be built on a PHP framework, responsive, followed up with feedback sessions.
